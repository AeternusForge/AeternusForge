**Aeternus** — a Latin word meaning *eternal, everlasting, immortal,* or *perpetual.*

**Forge** — a furnace or hearth where metals are heated and wrought; a smithy. A workshop where pig iron is transformed into wrought iron.

**AeternusForge** — a workshop where ideas are formed to last the test of time.

Welcome to the forge! Our mission is simple: help people remember that they have the ingenuity to build whatever they can dream up, as long as they have the drive to pursue their dreams. Part of the goal is to build a makerspace in my area to help people jump-start their dreams. Along the way, I'm going to share the projects that I'm building, to hopefully help inspire someone to follow theirs.

Here's what you can expect:

- Detailed descriptions of the projects, with links in case you want to build along
- Build videos on YouTube and Rumble
- Free and paid CAD files (mostly free)
- Tools to help learn essential concepts

## A little about me

Born and raised in Minnesota, I've been an engineer building automated equipment for the medical device industry for the last 10 years. I have a master's in mechanical engineering and a minor in electrical engineering. Before college I competed in RoboCup Search and Rescue, where a team designs and builds an automated vehicle to traverse a post-disaster situation.

I have a bad habit of collecting hobbies and skills — including archery, sailing, woodworking, lapidary, welding, working on cars, and coming up with ridiculous ideas that anyone who knows me would just say is classic me. I love knowledge and want to continue trying new things.

## Why am I doing this?

Now I bet you're asking, "Why are you doing this?" My answer is that somewhere along the way I lost sight of my love of making things. I've turned into a workaholic, and my love of adventure, learning, and the itch to tinker has suffered. Part of that has been work, part of it family commitments — but either way, I want to love what I do again, so hopefully this is my way of getting back.

I also want to leave something behind for future inventors, engineers, and tinkerers. When I started, I had no guide, reference, or contacts to help me figure out how to get going, so there was a lot of trial, error, electrical shocks, burns, and unintentional explosions… and maybe accidentally turning the house into a giant light organ because I wired something wrong. I digress.

I believe in trying to have the next generation be smarter than us, and in the saying "We stand on the shoulders of giants." So to whoever is reading this: I hope the knowledge helps you accomplish your goals in life, and I wish you the best in your future endeavors.

—Aeternus
